In mathematics, the art of asking questions is more valuable than solving problems. Georg Cantor (1845 - 1918)

Years ago, inspired by the quote above and How Emotions Are Made (a beautiful book by Lisa Feldman Barrett), I finally decided to leave the energy industry and join a very exciting club called Neuroscience. Hoping my math and programming skills would come useful, I started hanging out with the computational crew. If you want to read more, please head over to the about page for a fuller curriculum vitae.

Why the title: Neurology for Machines?
As a dynamic stability analyser working for a transmission system operators, I always struggled putting my job in layman terms for my friends and family. Years later, after giving an hour of exciting introduction to computational neuroscience, Nela (a friend) asked: so you will be a Neurosurgeon for Robots? 😀
Neurology for Machines will be my attempt (having Nela's mindset) to explain what I study.
